Conversation starters

Six questions, not one inspection.

  • Show me something cool you made with AI?
  • What's it good at? What does it get wrong?
  • How do you know when to trust what it says?
  • Has it ever said something that felt off?
  • What would you never use it for?
  • Want to build something together this weekend?

Guardrails

Four to settle early.

  • Companion chatbots. Built to keep talking. A wellbeing question, not a tech one.
  • Privacy is a habit. No names, photos, address, or secrets. If you wouldn't put it on a postcard, don't paste it.
  • Confident, not correct. Teach one reflex: "how do you know that?" then check.
  • Protect the effort. If they can't explain what they handed in, AI did too much.
Care, not alarm
Pocket guide
Better prompts in 5 letters

Be CLEAR with it.

C
Context. Who is this for, what's the situation.
L
Limits. What it should not do.
E
Expectations. What a good answer looks like.
A
Architecture. Ask for steps, lists, structure.
R
Roadmap. "Plan first, then build. Ask me before you start."
Start with C and R. The rest follows.

Confident, not certain.

You do not need to be the expert in the room. You need to be the grown-up who is willing to learn out loud, to get it wrong, and to stay curious next to your child.

The most experienced person in any AI room was a beginner eighteen months ago. So are you. So is your teenager. Sit in that together.
