Where is the gap?
South Zone · 8 Principals · 3 Sessions · Standalone Series

Over three sessions, eight Principals from the South Zone sit with tensions that animate our leadership in schools. Sign-up basis. Standalone series; not tied to any institutional programme.

How the Series Works

  1. Three sessions. One question per session: Generations, Agency, Principles.
  2. Three sub-questions per session. Two are provided as stimulus. The third is authored by the group on the night.
  3. One framework per session. Brief, named, used to open the discussion.
  4. One interactive card per session. Digital. Reflections submitted to a shared sheet and, optionally, projected anonymously as a group wall.
  5. Chatham House. Nothing leaves the room with names attached. Aggregated reflections are shared back to the group only.
  6. No deliverables. No action plans, no commitments. One provocation taken home is enough.

Session 1 · Generations

Question
What generational story are we still telling that no longer holds?

Sub-questions

  • What was formative for me that is foreign to the generation I now lead?
  • What is formative for them that remains opaque to us?
  • Co-authored in the room.

Framework: The Three Effects

From Bobby Duffy's generational sociology. What we read as generational difference is usually a mix of three effects:

  • Cohort effect. Truly shaped by when you were born: the technology you met first, the crises you lived through at a formative age.
  • Period effect. True for everyone alive right now, regardless of age: a pandemic, a platform, a geopolitical shift.
  • Life-course effect. True for whoever happens to be at that stage of life: young people have always been idealistic; middle-aged people have always felt time closing in.

Exercise: take a "kids these days" or "staff these days" story you have caught yourself telling. Run it through the three effects. Most such stories thin considerably.

Session 2 · Agency

Question
Where does agency live, and where does it die?

Sub-questions

  • When did I last feel my own agency most sharply? When least?
  • What structures in our schools cultivate agency? What structures quietly erode it, even with good intent?
  • Co-authored in the room.

Framework: Agency in Three Tenses

From Emirbayer and Mische (1998). Every act of agency draws on three temporal orientations:

  • Iterational. Drawing on the past: habits, routines, inherited patterns.
  • Practical-evaluative. Judgment in the live moment.
  • Projective. Imagining futures and committing toward them.

Exercise: diagnose a current initiative in your school through the three tenses. Which dominates? Where are teachers stuck in iterational mode? Where are students asked for projective agency without iterational grounding?

Caution

Agency talk can become responsibilisation — offloading systemic problems onto individuals. When we tell teachers to be more agentic, are we empowering them or thinning our own responsibility? Biesta's point. Hold the tension; do not resolve it.

Session 3 · Principles

Question
Which principles will outlast us, which must we let go, and which are being born?

Sub-questions

  • Which principle in my leadership has been genuinely non-negotiable, and why has it held?
  • Which principle am I clinging to that the next generation will rightly let go?
  • Co-authored in the room.

Framework: The Principles Compass

Four directional stances on what we hold as principle. This is the series' synthesis device.

True North

Timeless. What must always hold. Abandoning these hollows the vocation.

Emerging East

Nascent. What is being born on our watch. Sensed, not yet fully named.

Setting West

Waning. What to let go. Principles that served their season and now constrain.

Grounding South

Stable. What keeps us steady through change. Often unspoken; often most important.

Principals populate their own compass first. Then the group builds a shared compass together. Every principle placed carries an annotation: the condition under which I would revise this. Prevents ossification.

Facilitation

Two facilitators: yourself and one invited Principal. Split by function, not by segment.

Time & Protocol

Holds the space. Manages pacing. Opens and closes. Never interprets.

Depth & Provocation

Presses on answers. Offers the framework at the right moment. Brings the occasional reference.

Swap roles across the three sessions.

Aggregation

All cards submit to one Google Sheet via an Apps Script backend. The schema is identical across cards.

Per-session fields

  • Three open-text responses, one per sub-question.
  • Shift self-rating, 1–5: "Compared to when I walked in, my thinking on this has shifted."
  • Resonance: "One thing I picked up from another Principal that I want to carry."
  • Provocation: "One question I am taking home."

Series-level patterns to watch for

  • Shift trajectory across three sessions.
  • Themes recurring across provocations.
  • Cross-school resonance: whose contributions are cited most.
  • Where the shared Compass populates most densely.
